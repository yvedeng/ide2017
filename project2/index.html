Question 2:

Motivation:

The movitation for us is to figure out how has the weather changed over the past 100 years. It is rather difficult to see all the trends and the differences, therefore we need to visualize the data over the 100 years and observe the trends.

Methods:

We picked 3 cities: Copenhagen, Kaunas and Beijing as our data set. Due to the format reason, we need to preprocess the data. We did following things:

  1. Change the spaces to comma or tab;

    2. *Povilas used tsv to load the data therefore he changed the spaces into the tab while Yifang and Xiaolan used csv as what we learned from the lecture hence comma is the choice for this.

  2. Remove the dash sign from the headers;

    3. *We realized that d3 does not recognize the column name with the dash symbol, therefore we simply removed them.

  3. Replace the unknown number - 999.90 to 0 ;

    4. *We view this number as an error, therefore for some reasons we set it as 0. This might not make sense when a city has a lot 0 degrees in several months so that we can not easily see this number as an error. Fortunately, we do not have so much errors in our data set and the cities we picked are not cold enough like that. Besides, we only used the annual and the seasonal data, such as winter and summer, so even if the winter trend is a bit misleading, the annual data would still say something meaningful. The reason that we don't pick the spring and autumn data is they are too similar to be compared.

Second, line graphs are used to plot the temperature over years for the cities individually. The temperature trend of a city is mutually independent to any other city's, hence we did not put 3 trends in the same plot. To make the graph more interative, we use svg.on("mouseover") and svg.on("mouseon") to achieve the "pop up box" when you move your cursor on any of the point on the line. And this box would tell you more details such as the year, the specific temperature and so on.

Discussions:

Beijing: As we can see from the plot, from 1939 to 2017, the temperature over 78 years is very stable, but we still can see the temperature is getting a little higher. Before 1989, the annual temperature has never higher than 13 degree, however after this year, the temperature has reached the highest point at 14.14 in 2014. Through the comparision of the seasonal data, you can easily see the trend of the temperature in the winter gets higher and higher.

Copenhagen: The data of Copenhagen is well-reserved from 1980 to 2017. Even though the data is up and down in a relatively large span, the trend of the temperature is going higher in general.

Kaunas: The time span of City Kaunas is from 1922 to 2017. The interesting point is the temperature in winter is rather different year to year. Besides of the variation, we can still see the year with relatively high temperature is occured more frequently over recent years.

Conclusions:

To conclude all the observations, the global warming is indeed a real thing. It changes bit by bit, so slow that we can not even notice. However, by comparing the data over around 100 years, we can directly see what is going on.
