This website has 3 navigation bars, i.e. Browsing, Choose Your Room and Analysis. It aims to help users get a general picture of Airbnb situations at Copenhagen.

As a starting point for this visualization, we used data from the Airbnb at Copenhagen. Meanwhile, we used GeoJSON-formatted dataset to sketch the outline of districts, which is downloaded at Copenhagen data website. Note that 10 districts are covered by the geojson while Copenhagen actually has 11 districts. Since the data for district "Frederiksberg" is missing, there is no interaction at Frederiksberg on the map in page Browsing.

As people keep moving in and moving out everyday, according to statistical rules, the roughly general situation almost doesn't change with time flying when sample data is enough. Also, only data on the day of 15th June, 2017 is available. Based on this, we make a map plot to give the distribution of Airbnb housing over all 11 neighbourhoods, a filter to help readers get a perfect match in a proper range, and a stacked bar plot to compare the percentage of room types and price interval among 11 neighbourhoods.

The visualization is mainly built with d3.v4.js. The maps on Browsing part is built with Google developer API.
